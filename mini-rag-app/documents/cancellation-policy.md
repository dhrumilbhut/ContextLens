# Cancellation Policy

## Cancelling a Monthly Subscription

Monthly subscriptions can be cancelled at any time through the account settings page under Billing > Manage Subscription. There is no minimum notice period for monthly plans — you can cancel on the last day of a billing period and still avoid being charged for the next month. Once cancelled, the subscription remains active through the end of the current billing period and then lapses automatically; no further action is required.

## Cancelling an Annual Subscription

Annual subscriptions require at least 30 days' written notice before the renewal date to avoid being charged for the next year. Notice must be submitted through the account settings page or by emailing billing@example.com with your account ID. If the 30-day notice window is missed, the subscription will renew for another 12 months and refund eligibility will follow the standard annual refund policy (see Refund Policy).

## What Happens After Cancellation

After a subscription is cancelled, access to paid features is retained through the end of the billing period. At that point the account automatically downgrades to the free tier, if one is available, or access is suspended. Data associated with the account is retained for 90 days after the end of the paid period, after which it is subject to deletion under our data retention policy.

## Reactivating a Cancelled Subscription

A cancelled subscription can be reactivated at any time before data deletion occurs. If you reactivate within the same billing period in which you cancelled, the cancellation is reversed and you are not charged again until the next renewal date. If you reactivate after the billing period ends, a new billing cycle begins from the date of reactivation.

## Team and Enterprise Plans

Team and Enterprise plan cancellations must be submitted by an account owner or administrator, not a standard team member. Enterprise agreements may have specific contractual notice periods that override the standard 30-day policy — refer to your signed agreement for the exact terms.
