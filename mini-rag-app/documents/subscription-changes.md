# Subscription Changes: Upgrades, Downgrades, and Plan Switching

## Upgrading Your Plan

Plan upgrades take effect immediately. When you upgrade mid-cycle, we calculate the prorated cost for the remainder of the current billing period at the new plan's rate and charge the difference to your payment method on file. For example, if you upgrade halfway through a monthly billing period, you are charged approximately half the price difference between your old and new plan for that period. The next full billing cycle is then charged at the new plan rate.

## Downgrading Your Plan

Plan downgrades take effect at the start of the next billing period. You retain access to the higher-tier features and limits through the end of the current billing period, after which your account is adjusted to reflect the lower plan's features and resource limits. We do not issue prorated credits for downgrading mid-cycle. If your current usage (e.g. number of team members, storage used) exceeds the limits of the plan you are downgrading to, you will be prompted to reduce usage before the downgrade takes effect.

## Switching Between Monthly and Annual Billing

Switching from monthly to annual billing takes effect immediately. The annual price is charged in full at the time of the switch, and any remaining prepaid time from your current monthly cycle is credited toward the annual plan on a prorated basis. Switching from annual to monthly billing takes effect at the end of the current annual period — you cannot switch to monthly billing mid-year without cancelling the annual subscription.

## Plan Limits After Downgrade

If usage exceeds the limits of the downgraded plan at the time of downgrade (e.g. you have 10 team members but the lower plan allows only 5), access for excess members is suspended, not deleted. Suspended members regain access immediately if you upgrade again. Data associated with suspended members is retained for 60 days before being subject to deletion.

## Changing Plans on an Enterprise Contract

Enterprise customers with custom pricing agreements cannot self-serve plan changes through the billing portal. Contact your account manager to discuss plan modifications, which may involve a contract amendment.
